The 4th Morpeth Scout Group gives young people the oportunity to enjoy fun and adventure while developing the skills they need for life.

#SkillsForLife

What we do

4th Morpeth is a very popular Scout Group with over 250 young members in 3 Beaver Colonies, 4 Cub Packs and 4 Scout Troops. One of our Troops is due to be formed as a Sea Scout Troop, this is just a Scout Troop with a nautical theme and more water-based activities.

Our sections

  • Ages 6-8 years

    Make friends and try new indoor and outdoor activities

  • Ages 8-10½ years

    Learn practical skills while having adventures with friends

  • Ages 10½-14 years

    Build confidence, resilience and a sense of adventure

Other local groups

There is a another Scout Group in Morpeth called 6th Morpeth who meet at the Methodist Church Hall. They have Beavers, Cubs and Scouts on both Wednesdays and Thursdays. We can put you in touch with them.

There are further Scout Groups in Alnwick, Ashington, Ellington, Felton, Guidepost, Ponteland, Stakeford and Stannington.

Volunteer

Do more.

Share more.

Be more.

Each week, we help our Beavers, Cubs and Scouts develop the skills to succeed in life. But this can only happen thanks to our brilliant volunteers.

Volunteering is easier than you think. Whether you can spare an hour a month or a day a year, we have opportunities both behind the scenes and directly supporting young people. With full support and training, you can volunteer on a flexible basis.

Not only is it incredibly rewarding and great fun, you’ll learn some brilliant skills transferable to the workplace and further education.

We welcome volunteers from every community and background. You don’t need to have been a Scout. You just need to believe in a more positive future and the difference we can make together.

Volunteer today and help us prepare more young people with Skills for Life.

Find out more at scouts.org.uk/join/ or get in touch.
